About Me

Hi, I'm Tayiwa Bwala. I am passionate about creating meaningful impact through my work as a lawyer, researcher, content creator, and advocate for global change. I currently work at the Korean Embassy in Abuja, where I leverage my expertise in law and diplomacy to strengthen bilateral relationships, promote policy reforms, and explore opportunities for economic growth and sustainable development. Throughout my career, I have collaborated with think tanks, civil society organizations, and global institutions to address key issues such as enhancing citizen engagement in accountability and social inclusion, advancing public policy initiatives like the Credible Elections and Governance & Accountability programmes, as well as tackling climate change, economic diversification, and gender equity.

My work is driven by a commitment to finding innovative solutions that improve lives and strengthen communities. Beyond my professional pursuits, I am actively involved in community-building activities. As the National Special Duties Coordinator of the Youth Alive Fellowship (YAF) in Living Faith Church Worldwide, I lead initiatives to inspire and empower the next generation of leaders. My faith and dedication to service guides my efforts to create positive change both locally and globally.

I am also the creator of Tayiwa's Corner, a content platform where I share faith-based content, reflections, and insights on how serving God and living a life of purpose can lead to fulfillment and growth. Through platforms like YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook, I aim to inspire others, engage in conversations of hope, and build a community rooted in faith, positivity, and support. Looking to the future, I aspire to represent my country as an ambassador or work within international organizations like the United Nations to further contribute to a better, more equitable world.
